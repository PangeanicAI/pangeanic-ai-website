## Overview

Instruction-tuning datasets are structured examples used to adapt AI models to follow prompts, answer questions, comply with response formats and behave more reliably across downstream workflows. These datasets typically include prompt-response pairs, question-answer structures, task instructions and multi-turn conversational interactions.

Modern enterprise AI systems require more than generic web-scale corpora. Production-grade multilingual LLMs increasingly depend on supervised fine-tuning (SFT), instruction alignment, conversational tuning and domain-adapted instruction datasets capable of supporting real operational workflows. 

This repository documents Pangeanic’s instruction-tuning dataset capabilities for multilingual LLMs, conversational AI, enterprise copilots, RAG systems, domain-specific AI and sovereign multilingual AI initiatives.

Pangeanic structures instruction-tuning datasets as operational AI assets designed around multilingual governance, human-in-the-loop validation and enterprise deployment requirements. PECAT workflows support annotation, validation, anonymization, multilingual review and evaluation pipelines across instruction-tuning operations.
